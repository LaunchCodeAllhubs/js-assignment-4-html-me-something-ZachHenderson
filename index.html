Spider Records

Everyone knows at least one person in their life that is terrified of spiders. It may even be you, the reader, that gets a skin-crawling sensation whenever you see an arachnid. However, if you take the time to learn more about them, you may find that the fear is gradually replaced by intrigue. They are fascinating animals if you take the time to get to know them better.

  1. The largest spider in the world is the Giant Huntsman Spider, which can grow to be 12 inches in length.
  2. The smallest spider in the world is the Samoan moss spider at .011 inches.
  3. The fastest spider is Karaops, which can do a full 360° turn of its body 8 times within a second.
  4. The most venomous spider is the Sydney funnel-web spider.
  5. The strongest spider in relation to its bodyweight is the Californian trapdoor spider. It can hold its trapdoor closed against up to 38 times its own bodyweight.
  6. Vampire spiders feed upon Anopheles mosquitoes after they have sucked blood from a host.

Spiders in Mythology

Spiders have shown up in the myths of cultures around the world. Here are some examples.

  • Anansi

    • Anansi is from Akan (present-day Ghana) folktales.

  • Arachne

    • Arachne is from Greek myths. She started as a human, but was then cursed by Athena, and turned into a spider.
